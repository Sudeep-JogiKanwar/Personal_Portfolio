Forest Fire Risk Mapping Using GIS and AHP in Lumbini Province, Nepal

Overview

Developed a comprehensive forest fire risk assessment system for Lumbini Province, Nepal's third-largest province covering 974,000 hectares of forest ecosystems. This research addressed a critical knowledge gap by creating the first province-wide fire risk mapping study for the region.

Research Methodology

Literature review synthesis, systematic weight assignment, model validation

Methodology

Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis (MCDA)
  • Implemented Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) to systematically evaluate 11 causative factors
  • Conducted rigorous pairwise comparison analysis with consistency ratio of 0.04 (well below 0.1 threshold)
  • Integrated diverse datasets including satellite imagery, climate data, and topographic information
Geospatial Analysis
  • Primary Software: ArcMap 10.8, Python Google Earth Engine API
  • Data Integration: Combined multi-resolution datasets (30m-5.5km) from Landsat 8, MODIS, SRTM, and CHIRPS
  • Temporal Analysis: Temporal Analysis: Processed 25 years of climate data (2000-2025) and 1 year of fire incident data (2024-2025)
Key Causative Factors Analyzed
  • Biophysical: NDVI, land cover, elevation, slope, aspect, topographic wetness index
  • Anthropogenic: Distance from settlements and roads
  • Climatic: Land surface temperature, precipitation, wind speed

Results and Impact

Model Performance
  • Validated using 14,516 VIIRS fire incidents with nominal and high confidence levels
  • Achieved AUC value of 0.848, demonstrating high model reliability and accuracy
Key Findings
  • High-Risk Districts Identified: Palpa, Pyuthan, and Arghakhanchi require prioritized fire management
  • Risk Distribution: Created detailed spatial analysis showing district-wise fire vulnerability
  • Primary Risk Factors: LC (weight: 0.18) and NDVI (weight: 0.16) emerged as most significant
Practical Applications
  • Provides evidence-based decision support for local government agencies and forest managers
  • Enables proactive resource allocation for fire prevention infrastructure
  • Supports development of targeted fire management strategies for ecosystem resilience

Significance

This study represents the first comprehensive forest fire risk assessment for Lumbini Province, filling a critical research gap in Nepal's fire management capabilities. The integrated GIS-AHP methodology provides a replicable framework for fire risk assessment, contributing to both local fire management and broader disaster risk reduction strategies.
